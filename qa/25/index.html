What is an economizer and how are its controls configured?

Air-side#economizer#free-cooling#ASHRAE-90.1#damper

Air-Side Economiser Design and Control

An air-side economiser is one of the most cost-effective energy-saving measures available in commercial HVAC — it uses cool outdoor air in place of mechanical cooling whenever outdoor conditions are favourable. ASHRAE 90.1-2019 mandates economisers on most air handlers over 54,000 BTU/h (4.5 tons) cooling capacity in all but the hottest climate zones.

### Economiser Configurations

The economiser section contains three sets of linked dampers — outdoor air, return air, and relief/exhaust. As the economiser opens to admit more outdoor air, the return air damper proportionally closes and the relief damper opens to prevent building over-pressurisation. The three damper sets must be mechanically or electronically linked so the total airflow path resistance remains approximately constant regardless of mixing ratio, preventing supply fan surging.

### Changeover Strategies

The changeover control determines when the economiser can operate. The simplest and most common is **differential dry-bulb**: compare outdoor air dry-bulb temperature against return air temperature, and enable the economiser when outdoor is cooler. This is reliable but ignores humidity — in humid climates, cool but extremely moist outdoor air can increase the latent cooling load beyond what the sensible savings justify.

**Differential enthalpy control** compares total heat content (sensible + latent) using enthalpy sensors or calculated enthalpy from temperature and humidity, providing a more accurate changeover. **Fixed dry-bulb with adjustable setpoint** is simpler and common in dry climates, using a single outdoor temperature threshold (typically 18–21°C).

Per ASHRAE Guideline 36, the changeover setpoint should incorporate a 1°C deadband to prevent cycling, and the economiser should be locked out when the outdoor air dew point exceeds the supply air dew point setpoint — preventing the introduction of moist air that the cooling coil must then dehumidify.

### Integrated Economiser Control

The economiser operates in sequence with mechanical cooling. As zone cooling demand increases, the economiser damper opens from minimum position (set to meet ventilation requirements) towards fully open. Only when the economiser is fully open and additional cooling is needed does the mechanical cooling stage activate or modulate. This integrated sequence ensures maximum free cooling before any compressor energy is consumed.

### High-Limit and Low-Limit Protection

A mixed air low-limit sensor protects the cooling coil from freezing during cold-weather economiser operation. When mixed air temperature approaches 2–3°C, the economiser damper modulates towards minimum position regardless of cooling demand. The freeze-stat capillary sensor provides a redundant safety layer, directly tripping the fan and closing the outdoor damper if mixed air drops below 1.7°C.
