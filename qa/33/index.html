How do I perform an airflow traverse for duct commissioning?

Air-side#airflow#commissioning#traverse#ASHRAE-111

Performing an Airflow Traverse for Duct Commissioning

The airflow traverse is the gold-standard reference measurement for duct commissioning — it provides the baseline airflow data against which all permanently installed flow measuring stations are calibrated. Performed correctly, it achieves ±2–3% accuracy. Performed poorly, it can produce errors of 15–25% and compromise every downstream commissioning activity.

### The Log-Tchebycheff Method

ASHRAE Standard 111 prescribes the log-Tchebycheff method for determining measurement point locations in rectangular ducts. The duct cross-section is divided into equal-area rectangles (typically 5 rows × 5 columns = 25 points for ducts over 0.5 m²), and the measurement point within each rectangle is offset from the centre using log-Tchebycheff coefficients that account for the logarithmic velocity profile near duct walls. This produces more accurate results than simple centre-of-equal-area placement, particularly for the boundary layers where velocity changes most rapidly.

For round ducts, the traverse is performed along two perpendicular diameters with 6–10 measurement points per diameter (12–20 total). The measurement points are spaced closer together near the duct wall where velocity gradients are steepest.

### Equipment and Procedure

The essential equipment is a calibrated pitot-static tube connected to an electronic micro-manometer (readable to ±1 Pa) via flexible tubing. The pitot tube measures both total pressure (facing into the airflow) and static pressure (perpendicular ports), and the velocity pressure (ΔP = total − static) is converted to velocity using: v = k × √(2 × ΔP / ρ), where k is the pitot tube coefficient (typically 0.99–1.00) and ρ is the air density.

The critical procedural step — and the one most often skipped — is verifying that the duct static pressure is stable before beginning the traverse. If the supply fan is hunting or VAV boxes are modulating, the velocity profile will shift between measurement points, producing an unrepresentative average. The fan should be commanded to a fixed speed (via the VSD manual override) and all terminal boxes set to their commissioning positions before starting.

### Straight Duct Requirements

For the traverse to produce valid results, the measurement plane must have adequate straight duct upstream and downstream. ASHRAE Standard 111 requires 7.5 hydraulic diameters upstream (from the last disturbance — elbow, damper, takeoff) and 3 diameters downstream for accurate measurements. If these distances cannot be achieved, the traverse should still be performed but the number of measurement points should be increased by 50% to capture the non-uniform velocity profile. The compromised location must be documented in the commissioning report with an explanatory note.

### Calculation and Documentation

The velocity at each point is averaged (arithmetic mean of all point velocities, not a weighted average), and the average velocity is multiplied by the duct cross-sectional area to compute the volumetric airflow. If the barometric pressure or temperature differs significantly from standard conditions (20°C, 101.325 kPa), a density correction should be applied. The traverse result becomes the reference for calibrating the permanently installed airflow measuring station — typically by adjusting the station's k-factor until the station reading matches the traverse within ±5%.

🔑 Key Takeaways

  • ✓The log-Tchebycheff method provides ±2–3% accuracy for duct airflow measurement when performed with adequate straight duct and sufficient measurement points.
  • ✓Rectangular ducts require minimum 25 measurement points (5×5 grid); round ducts require 12–20 points across two perpendicular diameters.
  • ✓System airflow must be stabilised before the traverse — command the fan to a fixed speed and set VAV boxes to commissioning positions.
  • ✓If straight duct requirements cannot be met (7.5 diameters upstream, 3 downstream), increase measurement points by 50% and document the limitation.
  • ✓The traverse result becomes the reference for calibrating permanently installed flow measuring stations — document all raw data, not just the final airflow figure.
