What is the role of pressure sensors in duct static pressure reset strategies?

Air-side#static-pressure#sensor-placement#VAV#ASHRAE-36#energy

Duct Static Pressure Reset Strategy in Detail

The duct static pressure sensor is the single most important input for VAV fan energy optimisation. ASHRAE Guideline 36's trim-and-respond reset strategy transforms this sensor from a simple setpoint control device into the engine of a continuous energy optimisation algorithm.

### The Trim-and-Respond Algorithm

The algorithm is elegantly simple: every 2–5 minutes, the BMS controller polls the damper position of every VAV box on the system. If all dampers are below a threshold (typically 85–90% open), the static pressure setpoint is trimmed downward by a small increment — usually 0.1–0.2 in. w.g. (25–50 Pa). This process repeats continuously, so the setpoint "rides" just above the point where the most-demanding zone requires full airflow. When a zone's damper reaches 90–95% open, the algorithm stops trimming and may respond upward if multiple zones simultaneously demand more pressure.

### Energy Savings Magnitude

The fan power law states that fan power is proportional to the cube of speed, and speed is proportional to the square root of pressure. Therefore, reducing static pressure by 20% reduces fan power by approximately 36%. In a typical VAV system, a fixed setpoint of 1.5 in. w.g. might be reduced by the reset algorithm to 0.8 in. w.g. during part-load conditions — a 47% reduction in setpoint yielding roughly 65% fan energy savings during those hours. Since commercial buildings operate at part load for 80–90% of occupied hours, the annual savings are substantial.

### Sensor Placement and Accuracy

The static pressure sensor must be installed at the point that represents the pressure required by the hydraulically most remote VAV box. Industry consensus places this at 2/3 to 3/4 of the distance from the fan to the end of the main duct. The sensor probe must be oriented perpendicular to airflow (static pressure only, not total pressure) and installed in a straight duct section with 5–7 hydraulic diameters upstream and 2–3 diameters downstream.

### Controller Implementation

Most modern BMS platforms — including JCI Metasys, Siemens Desigo, and Schneider EcoStruxure — now ship with Guideline 36-compliant static pressure reset program blocks that can be configured with the polling interval, trim increment, and damper position threshold. The commissioning agent must verify that all VAV damper positions are accurately reported to the supervisory controller and that the static pressure sensor is calibrated to ±0.05 in. w.g.

🔑 Key Takeaways

  • ✓ASHRAE Guideline 36 trim-and-respond resets duct static pressure by polling VAV damper positions every 2–5 minutes and trimming the setpoint down in 0.1–0.2 in. w.g. increments
  • ✓Fan energy savings from static pressure reset can reach 30–65% during part-load conditions, which represent 80–90% of operating hours
  • ✓The sensor must be at 2/3 to 3/4 of the duct length with adequate straight duct runs (5–7 diameters upstream)
  • ✓Sensor calibration to ±0.05 in. w.g. is essential — trim increments of 0.1 in. w.g. cannot be meaningfully implemented with inaccurate sensors
  • ✓Modern BMS platforms ship pre-built Guideline 36 reset blocks; commissioning focus should be on verifying VAV damper position reporting accuracy
