How do I specify actuator runtime for different applications?

Actuators#runtime#timing#PID#selection

Matching Actuator Runtime to Process Dynamics

Actuator runtime — the time required for the actuator to travel from fully closed to fully open — is a critical but often overlooked specification that directly affects control loop stability and occupant comfort. An actuator that is too fast for its process will cause hunting and valve/damper wear; one that is too slow will fail to respond to load changes, causing temperature excursions.

### The Process Time Constant Principle

The fundamental rule is that actuator runtime should be approximately 20–30% of the process time constant (T63 — the time for the controlled variable to reach 63% of its final value after a step change in actuator position). For a VAV zone, the thermal time constant is typically 5–15 minutes (depending on space volume, internal loads, and airflow rate), so actuator runtimes of 60–90 seconds are appropriate. For a steam heating coil, the process responds in 30–90 seconds, so actuators of 15–30 seconds are required.

### Application-Specific Guidelines

**VAV Terminal Unit Dampers (60–90 seconds):** These are the most common runtime specification. The relatively slow runtime allows the DDC controller's PID loop to observe the effect of a damper change on zone temperature before issuing the next command. Faster runtimes (< 45 seconds) on VAV boxes often cause the damper to hunt between 10–30% open as the controller over-corrects before the zone temperature has responded.

**AHU Economiser Dampers (90–150 seconds):** Large mixing dampers with face areas of 2–10 m² require slower runtimes because the mixed air temperature responds slowly to changes in outdoor/return air ratio, and the large blade mass creates inertia that fast actuators cannot overcome smoothly. Belimo's standard recommendation is 150-second runtime for dampers over 4 m².

**Steam Valves (15–30 seconds):** Steam systems have very fast dynamics — opening a steam valve increases coil output almost immediately. A slow actuator on a steam valve will cause temperature overshoot because the valve continues opening after the controller has already detected the setpoint has been reached. Honeywell's steam valve selection tools default to 15–20 second runtimes for coils under 100 kW.

**Chilled Water Valves (60–120 seconds):** These fall between air-side and steam dynamics. The water volume in the coil and piping creates a thermal lag of 2–5 minutes, so actuator runtimes of 60–90 seconds for small coils and 90–120 seconds for large coils are appropriate.

🔑 Key Takeaways

  • ✓Actuator runtime should be approximately 20–30% of the process time constant (T63) for stable control loop performance
  • ✓VAV dampers need 60–90 second runtimes to match the 5–15 minute zone thermal time constant; faster actuators cause hunting
  • ✓Steam valves require the fastest actuators (15–30 seconds) because steam systems respond almost immediately to valve position changes
  • ✓Large AHU dampers over 4 m² need 120–150 second runtimes to manage blade inertia and slow mixed air temperature response
  • ✓Use Belimo SelectPro or manufacturer sizing tools that match runtime to application type automatically based on thermal time constant
